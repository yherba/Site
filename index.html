What it is

A second opinion at the shelf

You tell PourScore what you like in a cup. It takes the details off a coffee — the origin, process, roast, tasting notes — and gives you a plain read on how likely you are to enjoy it. No hype, no five-star theatre: when it doesn't have enough to go on, it says so.

Once a coffee's in your hands, you can log how each brew actually tasted and keep track of the recipe that worked best. Over time it becomes a quiet record of what you reach for and why.

PourScore also works with the gear you own. Set your grinder and brewer in Settings and its starting recipes adjust to match. Switch the brewer on any coffee and it recalculates a starting point for that method — a way to ask "how would I brew this on what I'm holding now?" — without touching the recipe you've saved as best.

Getting started

Your first ten minutes

Start by setting your coffee preferences. It takes about a minute and gives PourScore enough context to offer a read. After that, add a coffee manually or scan a bag.

  1. Set your taste preferences first. It takes a minute, and PourScore needs them to give you a read — skip it and the verdict comes back blank.
  2. Add two or three coffees manually. On the home screen tap “Add manually” and type in whatever's on a bag you've got — roaster, origin, process, roast level, tasting notes. Rough guesses are fine.
  3. Open one and look at its read. That's PourScore's best guess at whether it's a coffee you'd enjoy, before you commit to it.
  4. Log a brew. Jot down how it actually tasted. That's the part that gets smarter the more you use it.

Not sure what you like?

Start from the cup you'd choose

If specialty coffee is new to you, don't overthink it — pick the roast and the few flavors that sound like the cup you'd reach for. You can change it later, and it's fine to leave the rest blank for now.

Roast Light, delicate · medium, balanced · dark, bold and roasty
Flavors — tap any that sound good Fruity (berry, citrus, juicy) · Bright (lively, zippy) · Floral (delicate, tea-like) · Chocolatey (cocoa, comforting) · Nutty (almond, mellow) · Clean (crisp, easy-drinking) · Funky (wild, surprising)

Prefer smooth, low-acid coffees? Just leave Bright unselected.

Origin and process are in there too — but if those words don't mean much yet, skip them. They're easy to add once you know what you reach for.

Scanning a bag

Read a bag from a photo

Point your camera at a coffee bag and PourScore reads the details for you — roaster, origin, process, roast, tasting notes — so you don't have to type them in. No Anthropic account or API key needed: setup is just a one-time access code.

  1. Update to the latest TestFlight build.
  2. Open Settings in the app.
  3. Paste the access code from your TestFlight invite.
  4. Start scanning. Tap Scan, point at a bag, and PourScore fills in the details for you to check and save.

You can still add coffees by hand anytime — scanning is just the faster way in.

Your data

Where everything lives

PourScore keeps your library on your phone. There's no account to create and nothing to sign into.

  • Your coffees, preferences, and brew logs are stored only on your device.
  • Setup uses a one-time access code, saved on your device — there's no personal API key to manage.
  • When you scan a bag, the photo is sent to PourScore's scanning service, which passes it to Anthropic's AI to read the label and returns the details to your phone. That's the only time the contents of a scan leave your device, and only when you choose to scan.
  • Photos you attach stay in your own photo library; the app just points to them.
  • PourScore doesn't track you or run analytics.

Deleting a coffee removes it and its brew history from the app. Questions about any of this? Reach out below.
